Preshow

“Thirst” is the title of the 1931 propaganda film created by the Metropolitan Water District of Southern California to scare voters into approving funding for a 400-mile long aqueduct from the Colorado River to the City of Los Angeles. While this boring political mind control film will not be screened, it is a good example of the zeitgeist of water scarcity and the deep seeded roots of the theme of “thirst” in California. The preshow will give some context to the past and present water struggles, hopefully enhancing the experience of the feature film. The original audience of Chinatown viewed a past water crisis while in the midst of their own water crisis; today in the future (relatively speaking), the crisis persists, and in the future, the crisis will likely persist.

If you are interested in a more indepth look at Southern California's water resource history, read this lecture given by Martha Davis at UCLA for an Environmental Symposium. Its a little out of date (1998), but she gives a nice concise history with an optimistic outlook on the future.
