Vesturbaer Cinema presents Alejandro Jodorowsky’s The Holy Mountain

Born in Chile to Russian immigrants, educated in Paris, and conceptual conqueror of Mexico, Jodorowsky created a new path of surrealism through the 60’s and 70’s… and then doused that path in gasoline and fire, leaving us all burning, suffering, and screaming in his wake. The Holy Mountain transcends the psychedelic film experience of the 70’s, challenging reality and the pursuit of redemption in a world of blood thirsty war and enormous enterprise.

The Theif, a Christ-like figure, encounters The Alchemist, who leads him and 7 other power hungry leaders on a pilgrimage to the Holy Mountain. To reach the edge where land meets heavens, they must liberate themselves from their temporary mortal identities to obtain immortality and become gods.

Jodorowsky’s work is simply incapable of being anything less than “challenging,” and The Holy Mountain is no cake walk. As stated in the trailer, “Nothing in your education or experience can have prepared you for this film.” Ripe with vulgarity, and soaking with enough sacrilege to make the Pope cry tears of blood, this film explores a journey to enlightenment from a dirty, scary and hauntingly familiar place. Coming out of an era where the answer to a film was commonly found in mind altering drugs and experimental spirituality, Jodorowsky still leaves us with few answers to the questions we do not even know how to ask. Still, and foremost, The Holy Mountain is a rich, visceral, humorous, provocative and sometimes straight up offensive visual experience, financially made possible by the likes of John Lennon.

Reality is about to become rather debatable. Let us indulge in the illusion of it. Maybe it will all make sense after you cross this cult favorite off your bucket list. Maybe not. Who cares anyway? Not Jodorowsky.
