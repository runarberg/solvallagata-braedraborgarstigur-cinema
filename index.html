Some of our staff members (that is our AV-technician) need their holidays (or their holiday pay to be precise). Therefor there won’t be any show this week until the monday after easter.

This week’s preshow theme is Colonialism. The selected clips will present the multidimensional grandiosity of the colonial mind composed of the self-righteous oppressor, self-proclaimed savior, humble servant, and a barrel of laughs. We are happy to announce that the preshow will include a delightful 1961 cartoon by power couple Charles and Ray Eames (we want to be just like them when we grow up!).

The Man Who Would Be King is a 1975 film adapted from the Rudyard Kipling short story of the same title. It was adapted and directed by John Huston and starred Sean Connery, Michael Caine, Saeed Jaffrey, and Christopher Plummer as Kipling (giving a name to the short story's anonymous narrator). The film follows two rogue ex-non-commissioned officers of the Indian Army who set off from late 19th-century British India in search of adventure and end up as kings of Kafiristan […]
